A linear form, also known as a linear functional, a one-form, or a covector, is a linear map from a vector space $V$ to its field of scalars.

Suppose that vectors in the real coordinate space \R^n are represented as column vectors \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1\\ \vdots\\ x_n\end{bmatrix}.

For each row vector \mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix}a_1 & \cdots & a_n\end{bmatrix} there is a linear functional f_{\mathbf{a}} defined by f_{\mathbf{a}}(\mathbf{x}) = a_1 x_1 + \cdots + a_n x_n, and each linear functional can be expressed in this form.
